Main Fork of the Salmon River, Idaho

The Salmon River in the Frank Church Wilderness of No Return, known as the "River of No Return" due to its rugged beauty that captivates visitors. The area is rich in wildlife, including elk, deer, bears, and bighorn sheep, and it features towering ponderosa pine trees along the riverbanks, offering shelter. The river has rapids ranging from class I to IV, and it is one of the longest undammed rivers in the United States. The water level varies with the seasons, with higher, faster, and colder water in early summer and lower, slower, and warmer water in late summer, making it suitable for families with children who enjoy swimming.

Desolation and Gray Canyon, Utah

Desolation Canyon in central Utah is an 84-mile stretch of the Green River leading to the Colorado River and the sea. It features stunning desert landscapes, high grey stone cliffs, and exhilarating rapids. The journey spans from Sand Wash to Swaseys Rapid near Green River, Utah, offering remarkable scenery, fascinating geologic formations, traces of prehistoric and historic human activity, and excellent whitewater opportunities. Desolation Canyon is classified as Class II/III rapids and typically takes 6 days to complete. It has received National Landmark status due to its unique features and rich historical significance.

Grand Canyon, Arizona

The Grand Canyon is the ultimate destination for river adventures, offering a combination of world-class whitewater and awe-inspiring scenery. It promises an unforgettable experience, with thrilling rapids and the opportunity to explore side canyons and ancient Indian ruins that are accessible only by river. The Grand Canyon uses a unique river rating scale from Class I to X (1 to 10) to accommodate the wide range of rapids and river conditions, although many people are more familiar with the standard I to V rating scale.
